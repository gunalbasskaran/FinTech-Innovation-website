How SharePlate Started

In the summer of 2024, our founder Maya Rivera noticed something troubling. After every neighborhood block party, bags of perfectly good food were tossed into the trash. Down the street, a family was struggling to put meals on the table.

Maya started a simple group chat for neighbors to post surplus food before throwing it away. Within a week, 40 households had joined. Within a month, the idea had spread to three more blocks. That grassroots experiment became SharePlate.

Today, we're a growing platform serving over 12,000 members — but our mission hasn't changed. We exist to make sure good food finds a good home, and neighbors find each other.

What We Stand For

The principles behind every feature, partnership, and design decision we make.

Community First

Everything starts with neighbors helping neighbors. We build tools that strengthen local bonds, not replace them with algorithms.

Radical Transparency

Our impact numbers, food safety guidelines, and business model are fully public. We believe trust is built by letting everyone see the work.

Zero Judgment

Sharing food isn't charity — it's community. Whether you're giving or receiving, you're playing an equal role in reducing waste.

Sustainable by Design

Every design choice — from local-only listings to low-data architecture — is made with environmental sustainability at the center.
